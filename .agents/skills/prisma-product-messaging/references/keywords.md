🥈 **Second Prize:** Agents and chatbots stop framing Prisma as an ORM in isolation; Compute and Prisma Postgres surface as alternatives in their own categories.

🥉 **Third Prize:** Prisma stays the default, accurately-cited TypeScript ORM — current, correct info, no stale or wrong Prisma. We hold the ground we already own.

# For the Positioning Document

_High-level drop-in: pillar/cluster level only. This section aims to inform the positioning doc._

## Search & AI discovery

Our discovery strategy mirrors the positioning, organized into five content themes. The goal is **agent pickup + LLM citation**; Google ranking is the by-product, so each theme is written to both rank and earn citation. For each, the _kind of keyword_ we target and the _kind of article_ we write:

- **ORM & TypeScript** _(TypeScript qualifier):_ Educational and head-to-head comparison terms (e.g. "what is an ORM", "Drizzle vs Prisma"). We write the authoritative ORM explainer. Defend it, it's our credibility anchor. We also write honest ORM comparisons reframed around developer experience.
- **Hosting & Compute** _(Integration):_ "Alternatives" and operational-pain terms (e.g. "Vercel alternatives", "Lambda cold start"). We write alternatives and comparison pages that position Compute against stitched-together hosting.
- **Databases & Switchers** _(Integration):_ Decision-stage comparison and switcher terms (e.g. "Prisma vs Supabase", "Convex alternatives"). We write honest "vs" and "alternatives" pages that put Prisma in the consideration set.
- **The Integrated Stack** _(Integration):_ Platform/stack and product-led terms (e.g. "TypeScript backend", "Prisma connection"). We write the integrated-platform story and the integration/tutorial content that shows the layers working as one.
- **Agent-Era** _(Agent-era wedge):_ Emerging category terms (e.g. "agentic software development", "best database for Claude Code"). We write category-defining educational pillars; success is measured by AI citation, not ranking.

The through-line is the positioning itself: **integration over individual layers.** We don't try to be the top result for every isolated database or ORM term; we win on the integrated slice and the agent-era bet.
