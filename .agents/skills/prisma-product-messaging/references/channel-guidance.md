# Channel guidance

Adjust the same product truth to the channel. Do not change the underlying claims.

## Universal rules

- Start with the reader's problem or task.
- Keep product naming exact.
- Use one clear idea per sentence.
- Remove em dashes, dramatic fragments, and empty slogans.
- Prefer one concrete workflow over three abstract benefits.
- If the channel rewards brevity, shorten the copy without inflating it.

## Docs

Docs should be:

- Clear.
- Accurate.
- Direct.
- Helpful.
- Specific.
- Step-by-step when needed.

Docs should avoid:

- Launch hype.
- Category claims.
- Unsupported benefits.
- Vague value statements.

## Website copy

Website copy should be:

- Benefit-led.
- Easy to scan.
- Audience-specific.
- Confident and specific.

Website copy should avoid:

- Generic SaaS phrases.
- Broad "all your infrastructure" claims.
- Benefits that are not tied to a workflow.

## Blog and educational content

Blog and content drafts should be:

- Educational.
- Practical.
- Grounded in examples.
- Human and readable.
- Clear about the user problem.

Blog and content drafts should avoid:

- Trying to sound profound.
- Restating the headline without adding detail.
- Turning every paragraph into a sales pitch.

## Launch copy

Launch copy should be:

- Clear about what changed.
- Honest about scope, maturity, and audience.
- Specific about why the change matters.
- Concrete when teasing upcoming work.

Launch copy should avoid:

- Announcing a category shift without proof.
- Making maturity claims that the product docs do not support.
- Repeating "fast", "simple", or "powerful" without evidence.
- Standalone teaser lines like `Something big is coming` or `More soon`.

For teaser sections:

- Make the heading about the area that is changing.
- Keep the teaser body to 1 to 2 complete sentences.
- Say where people should watch for updates, such as X, LinkedIn, Discord, or other Prisma channels.
- Do not imply a release date or scope you cannot support.

## X

X posts should be:

- Short.
- Specific.
- Human.
- Easy to understand without a long thread.

X posts should avoid:

- Empty one-liners.
- Forced suspense.
- Generic engagement bait.
- Over-packed feature lists.

## LinkedIn

LinkedIn posts should be:

- Slightly more contextual than X.
- Professional without sounding corporate.
- Focused on a practical insight, launch explanation, or user problem.

LinkedIn posts should avoid:

- Thought-leadership cliches.
- Self-congratulatory launch language.
- Long setup that delays the useful point.

## Customer-facing and support-style communication

Customer-facing responses should be:

- Calm.
- Helpful.
- Honest about limitations.
- Specific about next steps.

Customer-facing responses should avoid:

- Marketing language.
- Defensive tone.
- Vague promises.
- Hiding uncertainty.
