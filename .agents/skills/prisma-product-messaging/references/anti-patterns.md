# Writing anti-patterns

These patterns often make Prisma communication less clear, less credible, or less useful.

## Em dashes

### Weak example

Prisma Postgres gives you a managed database, and Prisma Compute handles app hosting, all in one platform.

### Why it is weak

The punctuation adds drama without adding meaning. It also conflicts with the requested style guardrail for this skill set.

### Better pattern

Use commas, periods, or parentheses. Keep the sentence direct.

### Better example

Prisma Postgres gives you a managed database, and Prisma Compute handles app hosting in the same platform.

## Empty slogans

### Weak example

Build. Deploy.

### Why it is weak

The line is too generic. It could describe almost any developer tool, so it does not help the reader understand Prisma.

### Better pattern

Name the workflow, product, or user outcome.

### Better example

Create a Prisma Postgres database, connect it to your app, and manage it through the same Prisma workflow you already use for schema changes.

## Dramatic fragments

### Weak example

The backend problem. Finally solved.

### Why it is weak

Short fragments create drama, but they do not explain what changed, for whom, or why the claim is credible.

### Better pattern

Use a full sentence that ties the problem to a product workflow.

### Better example

Prisma helps TypeScript teams manage schema, database, and deployment workflows in one platform instead of stitching them together across separate tools.

## Repetition for drama

### Weak example

It deploys fast. Very fast.

### Why it is weak

Repeating the claim makes the line sound polished, but it still does not explain what becomes faster or how the workflow improves.

### Better pattern

Replace repetition with a measurable or workflow-specific detail.

### Better example

Deploy the app and database workflow from the same codebase instead of moving between separate hosting and database tools.

## Generic SaaS claims

### Weak example

Built for developers who move fast.

### Why it is weak

The phrase is broad and interchangeable. It says nothing about Prisma's TypeScript, agent, or integration story.

### Better pattern

Tie the claim to the audience, problem, and specific Prisma advantage.

### Better example

Built for TypeScript teams that want their agent to handle schema, database, and deployment work without coordinating separate vendors.

## Unsupported superlatives

### Weak example

The best platform for every backend team.

### Why it is weak

It overreaches and is not supported by the repo sources. It also erases the actual target audience and anti-positioning.

### Better pattern

Use narrower, provable positioning.

### Better example

Prisma is a strong fit for TypeScript teams that want ORM, Postgres, and compute workflows to work together in one platform.

## Vague speed claims

### Weak example

Ship faster than ever before.

### Why it is weak

The sentence does not identify which step got faster or why Prisma changes the workflow.

### Better pattern

Explain the slowed step and the specific improvement.

### Better example

Use Prisma's managed database workflow so your team spends less time provisioning infrastructure and more time shipping application changes.

## Overused adjectives and intensifiers

### Weak example

Prisma gives you a powerful, seamless, effortless developer experience.

### Why it is weak

The adjectives stack up without proof. They ask the reader to trust a feeling instead of understand a concrete benefit.

### Better pattern

Replace adjectives with the workflow detail that earns them.

### Better example

Prisma keeps schema, database, and deployment workflows in one place, which reduces setup steps and cross-vendor debugging.

## Vague developer-experience claims

### Weak example

Prisma offers a better developer experience.

### Why it is weak

The phrase is empty unless the copy explains what becomes easier, safer, or clearer for the developer.

### Better pattern

Name the experience improvement in operational terms.

### Better example

Prisma gives TypeScript teams a schema-first workflow with generated client code and programmable platform surfaces, so fewer setup and query issues are discovered late.

## Empty teaser language

### Weak example

Something big is coming.

### Why it is weak

It creates suspense, but gives the reader almost no useful information. It sounds promotional without helping them understand what area is changing or why they should care.

### Better pattern

Name the area that is improving, keep the promise narrow, and close with a complete sentence about where updates will appear.

### Better example

Better Prisma Console navigation is on the way as part of a broader upcoming release. We will share more soon, so keep an eye on our social channels.
