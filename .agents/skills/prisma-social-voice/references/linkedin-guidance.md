# LinkedIn guidance

Use this for Prisma posts on LinkedIn.

## What good looks like

- Slightly more context than X.
- Professional, but still direct.
- Focused on a lesson, launch explanation, user pain, or practical takeaway.
- Grounded in one clear narrative.

## What to avoid

- Thought-leadership filler.
- Self-congratulatory launch language.
- Long openings that delay the useful point.
- Generic "developer productivity" claims without examples.
- Vague teaser copy that builds suspense but does not explain what area is improving.

## Better patterns

- Open with the problem or observation.
- Explain what Prisma changes.
- Close with the implication for the target audience.
- If teasing future work, mention the product area and give a complete next-step sentence such as where to watch for updates.

## Example shift

Weak:

We are redefining modern development for teams everywhere.

Better:

Many TypeScript teams still stitch together an ORM, a database, and hosting across separate vendors. Prisma's platform story is stronger when we explain how those workflows fit together, not when we reach for abstract transformation language.
